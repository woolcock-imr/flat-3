Exclusion Criteria
Do they have a medical condition or take medication that is the cause of their insomnia?
Do they have a diagnosis of sleep apnea or sleep-related movement disorder?
Do they have delayed sleep phase syndrome?
Are they shift-workers?
Have they travelled overseas (over 2 timezones) in the past month?
Do they have a known hypersensitivity to cannabis?
Do they have a known hypersensitivity to peppermint oil or medium-chain triglycerides (MCT) oil?
Do they have a past or present history of cannabis dependence?
Do they have a history of drug or alcohol dependence or abuse within the past 2 years?
Is there current evidence of clinically significant disease (including lack of capacity or significant cognitive decline) that could affect their safety or interfere with the study assessments?
Do they have a history of major psychiatric disorder in the past 12 months?
Do they have a history of suicide attempts or current suicidal ideation?
If participant is female, are they pregnant or lactating?
Have they used any modality of treatment for insomnia, including CBT, within 2 weeks before screening?
Have they used any CNS-active drugs in the past 3 months?
Are they on any medications that may have a clinically significant impact on the metabolism and excretion of cannabinoids?
Are they able to refrain from alcohol consumption for 24 hours prior to each treatment session?
Is their insomnia caused by excessive use of caffeine?
Are they able to refrain from caffeine use for 24 hours prior to each treatment session?
Are they a regular smoker?
Do they have a condition that results in frequent need to get out of bed? (e.g. nocturia)
Are they able to undergo MRI brain imaging?
Do they have any medical condition that can result in an abnormal EEG?
Are they required to complete mandatory drug testing? (e.g. workplace testing or court order)
Have they self-reported using cannabis within the past 3 months?
Have they tested positive to cannabis or any other drug of abuse on the urinary drug screen?
